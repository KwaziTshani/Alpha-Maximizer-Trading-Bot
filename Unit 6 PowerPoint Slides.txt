In February of 1990, the PAC and ANC were unbanned, Nelson Mandela
was released and the referendum in 1992 indicated that it was time for a
new negotiated settlement: one that converted SA from a non-democratic
state to a full democracy. Approximately 26 parties were involved in multiparty negotiations at the World Trade centre and as a result, the interim or
1993 constitution was adopted. On 27 April 1994, the first truly democratic
election was held and the interim constitution came into effect. This
constitution was a definite break from previous constitutions in many ways
